# Curriculum Mapping: CE2 (Cours Elementaire 2) -- Age 8

## Level Overview

- **French:** Cycle 2, Year 3 (last year of Cycle 2). Reference "attendus de fin de cycle 2" from BO n40, Oct 2024.
- **UK:** Year 3 (lower sub-levels 1-5) and Year 4 (upper sub-levels 6-10). Year 4 has the statutory Multiplication Tables Check (MTC).
- **Key objectives:** Numbers to 10,000, ALL multiplication tables x2-x9 memorized, column multiplication, division introduction, fractions (halves, thirds, quarters, fifths, tenths), perimeter, right angles.

---

| Topic Code | Topic Name | Scope |

**CE2-PRB: Problem Solving (2-step problems, operation selection)**

| Parameter | Micro 1 | Micro 3 | Micro 5 | Micro 7 | Micro 10 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| Operations | Addition + subtraction | Add/sub + multiplication | All 4 operations | All 4 operations | All 4 operations |
| Steps | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2-3 | 2-3 |

| Operation selection | Given | Given or choose | Choose | Choose | Choose + justify |
| Context variety | School, food | + shopping | + measurement | + time, money | All contexts |
| Distractors (for MC) | Wrong operation | Wrong step order | Partial answer | Plausible intermediate | All error types |

**Example questions:**
- Micro 1: "Emma a 245 billes. Elle en gagne 178 puis en perd 96. Combien en a-t-elle ?" Step 1: 245 + 178 = 423. Step 2: 423 - 96 = 327. Answer: 327
- Micro 5: "Un boulanger fait 8 fournees de 36 croissants. Il en vend 250. Combien lui en reste-t-il ?" Step 1: 8 x 36 = 288. Step 2: 288 - 250 = 38. Answer: 38
- Micro 10: "Une ecole achete 6 cartons de 48 cahiers et 4 cartons de 36 crayons. Combien d'articles en tout ?" Step 1: 6 x 48 = 288. Step 2: 4 x 36 = 144. Step 3: 288 + 144 = 432. Answer: 432

**Common mistakes for distractors:**
- Only completing step 1 (answering 423 instead of 327)
- Wrong operation in step 1 (subtracting instead of adding/multiplying)
- Computing correctly but answering the intermediate result
- Misidentifying which operation to use ("en gagne" -> subtraction error)

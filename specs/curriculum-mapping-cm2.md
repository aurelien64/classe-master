# Curriculum Mapping: CM2 (Cours Moyen 2)

## 7. CM2 (Cours Moyen 2) -- Age 10 -- UK Year 5-6

### 7.1 Level Overview

- **French:** Cycle 3, Year 2. Last year of primary school before 6eme (college). Reference BO Cycle 3 programme (BO n16, April 2025) and "attendus de fin de CM2".
- **UK:** Year 5 (lower SLs 1-5) and Year 6 (upper SLs 6-10). Year 6 includes SATs preparation.
- **Key objectives:** Numbers to 1 billion, all operations with large numbers, decimal operations (to thousandths), fraction operations (add/sub different denominators, multiply), proportionality, percentages introduction, divisibility criteria, volume, angles, complex multi-step problems. This level must prepare students for 6eme/secondary school.

**Weekly schedule alignment:**
- French BO: ~5 hours/week mathematics, minimum 2/3 on numbers/calculation/problem-solving.
- UK: ~5-6 hours/week mathematics (Year 5-6), daily maths lesson.

**End-of-level expectations (attendus de fin de CM2):**
- Read, write, compare, order whole numbers to 1,000,000,000 and decimals to thousandths.
- Fluent written and mental calculation with all four operations on whole numbers and decimals.
- Fractions: compare, add/subtract with different denominators, multiply fraction by integer.
- Proportionality: solve problems using ratio tables, unit rates, scaling.
- Geometry: measure and classify angles with protractor, calculate area of triangles/parallelograms, understand volume.
- Measurement: convert fluently between all metric units, understand compound units.
- Problem solving: multi-step problems across all domains with clear mathematical reasoning.

**Topic scope summary:**

- **CM2-NUM:** Numbers to 1,000,000,000, large number reading/writing/comparing/ordering, place value to billions, negative numbers introduction, rounding to any place.
- **CM2-ADD:** Addition and subtraction of decimals (to thousandths) and large whole numbers, mental strategies for efficient calculation, inverse operations for checking.
- **CM2-MUL:** Multi-digit multiplication including decimals, 3-digit x 2-digit column method, multiply by 10/100/1000 with decimals, estimation.
- **CM2-DIV:** Division with 2-digit divisor, decimal quotients, long division algorithm, divisibility criteria for 2/3/4/5/9/10, remainders as decimals or fractions.
- **CM2-FRA:** Add/subtract fractions with different denominators (denom <= 60), multiply fraction by integer, fraction x fraction introduction, equivalences, simplification, mixed numbers.
- **CM2-DEC:** Decimals to thousandths, all 4 operations with decimals, ordering and comparing, rounding, fraction-decimal links.
- **CM2-PRO:** Ratio tables, scaling, unit rates, cross-multiplication introduction, percentages of amounts, simple proportion problems.
- **CM2-GEO:** Angle measurement with protractor, triangle properties and classification, circle circumference (diameter x pi introduction), area of triangles/parallelograms, coordinate plotting (first quadrant), transformations.
- **CM2-MSR:** All metric unit conversions (length/mass/capacity), volume in cm3/m3/L, compound units introduction (speed, density awareness), scale drawings.
- **CM2-PRB:** Complex multi-step problems combining all domains, data interpretation from charts/graphs/tables, logic problems, algebraic thinking introduction.

##### CM2-NUM (Numbers & Place Value) -- SL1

**Focus:** Review numbers to 999,999. Read, write, compare, order. Place value units through hundred-thousands. Rounding to nearest 10, 100, 1000, 10000.

| Time per question | 15s | 12s | 12s | 15s | 12s |

**Example questions:**

- **ML1:** "Quel est ce nombre : soixante-douze mille quatre cent trente-cinq ?" MC: A) 72,435 B) 70,435 C) 72,345 D) 724,35 *[Answer: A]*
- **ML5:** "Range ces nombres du plus petit au plus grand : 456,200 -- 45,620 -- 465,200 -- 45,062" *[Answer: 45,062 < 45,620 < 456,200 < 465,200]*

- Confusing place value names (dizaines de mille vs centaines de mille).

- When rounding, looking at the wrong digit (e.g., rounding 847,562 to nearest 10,000 but looking at the hundreds digit instead of the thousands digit).
- Ordering errors when numbers have different digit counts (thinking 99,999 > 100,000 because "9 is bigger than 1").

**Example questions:**

- **ML1:** "Un produit coute 75 EUR. Il est d'abord solde a -20%, puis une remise supplementaire de 10% est appliquee. Quel est le prix final ?" *[Answer: 75 x 0,8 = 60 EUR. 60 x 0,9 = 54 EUR]*
- **ML5:** "Pour une recette de 6 personnes, il faut 450 g de farine, 3 oeufs et 75 cL de lait. Adapte pour 10 personnes." *[Answer: Facteur = 10/6 = 5/3. Farine: 450 x 5/3 = 750 g. Oeufs: 3 x 5/3 = 5 oeufs. Lait: 75 x 5/3 = 125 cL]*
- **ML10:** "Deux villes sont distantes de 420 km. Un TGV les relie en 1h45 et un TER en 4h30. Calcule la vitesse moyenne de chaque train. Le TGV est combien de fois plus rapide que le TER ? **Common mistakes:**
- Successive discounts: applying to the original price instead of the reduced price.
- Recipe scaling with non-integer factors: fraction multiplication errors.
- Speed: converting h:min to decimal hours incorrectly (1h45 = 1,75h, not 1,45h).
- "How many times faster": confusing ratio with difference.

### 7.4 Level-Up: Bridge to Secondary

#### What "Completing CM2" Means

Completing CM2 (reaching SL10 ML10 across all topics) signifies that a student has mastered the entirety of the French primary school mathematics curriculum (Cycles 2 and 3 combined) and the UK Key Stage 2 curriculum through Year 6. This student is fully prepared to enter 6eme (college) in the French system or Year 7 in the UK system.

**This is the most significant milestone in the game** -- the completion of primary school mathematics.

#### Skills Checklist for 6eme Readiness

**Numbers & Calculation:**
- [ ] Read, write, compare, order whole numbers to 1,000,000,000
- [ ] Read, write, compare, order decimals to 3 decimal places (thousandths)
- [ ] Add/subtract any whole numbers or decimals fluently (written and mental)
- [ ] Multiply multi-digit numbers including decimals
- [ ] Divide by 2-digit divisors with decimal quotients
- [ ] Apply all divisibility criteria (2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 10)
- [ ] Use order of operations (BIDMAS) correctly
- [ ] Know all multiplication tables to 9x9 (or 12x12 for UK) automatically
- [ ] Identify prime numbers, square numbers, cube numbers
- [ ] Understand and use negative numbers

**Fractions:**
- [ ] Add and subtract fractions with different denominators (denom up to 60)
- [ ] Multiply a fraction by a whole number
- [ ] Multiply a fraction by a fraction (basic cases)
- [ ] Simplify fractions using GCF
- [ ] Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- [ ] Solve problems involving fractions of amounts

**Proportionality & Percentages:**
- [ ] Recognize and solve proportional situations
- [ ] Use ratio tables, unit rates, cross-multiplication
- [ ] Calculate percentages of amounts (any percentage)
- [ ] Calculate percentage increase and decrease
- [ ] Understand and use ratio notation
- [ ] Share amounts in given ratios

**Geometry:**
- [ ] Classify and construct 2D shapes with precise measurements
- [ ] Measure and draw angles using a protractor
- [ ] Calculate missing angles (line = 180, point = 360, triangle = 180, quadrilateral = 360)
- [ ] Calculate area of rectangles, triangles, parallelograms
- [ ] Understand circumference and area of circles (using pi = 3.14)
- [ ] Plot and read coordinates in all 4 quadrants
- [ ] Perform translations and reflections on coordinate plane
- [ ] Identify and use nets of 3D shapes

**Measurement:**
- [ ] Convert fluently between all metric units (length, mass, capacity)
- [ ] Understand and convert area units (cm2, m2, km2)
- [ ] Calculate volume of cuboids in cm3, dm3, m3, and litres
- [ ] Convert between volume and capacity units
- [ ] Use compound units (speed in km/h and m/s)
- [ ] Work with scale drawings and maps

**Problem Solving:**
- [ ] Solve multi-step problems (4+ steps) combining multiple domains
- [ ] Interpret data from tables, bar charts, line graphs, pie charts
- [ ] Calculate mean (average)
- [ ] Use algebraic thinking (simple equations, formulae)
- [ ] Justify reasoning and explain mathematical thinking
- [ ] Estimate answers before calculating and verify after
